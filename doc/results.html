1 Analysis

1.1 VerticeDegrees.java

The degree computation on each graph had a runtime of Θ(3V). Since each call to inDegree/outDegree was O(V) and all V vertices were tested and queried for their degrees.

The storage space for the test was simple as nothing but a few ints and doubles were required, other than the matrix.

1.2 StronglyConnectedComponents.java

Calculating the SCCs entailed two calls to DFS and two sorts. Since java's sort in the Arrays class is advertised as O(V log V), and a DFS on an Adjacency Matrix is O(V2) the runtime is bounded by the two calls to DFS, which makes it O(V2).

The storage space uses two HashTables of O(V) and two Matrices of O(V + E). Therefore the algorithm's space complexity is bounded by O(V + E).

2.5.3 Strongly Connected Components

I was unable to get results running the program even if I set the -Xmx2028M argment. Considering the storage space for the program was 2(V * E) where |V| = 12833 and |E| = 38933, I would admit that my implementation has a bad storage bound, since I store both two HashMaps and an array of integers. I would say that my choice of Adjacency Matrices, despite their fast look-up rate, is not good for graphs as large and sparse.
